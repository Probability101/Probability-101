Key Definitions

Now before you start reading this page, you should be familiar with these definitions as they will be used throughtout the website and are essential in the world of statistics and more specifically probability.

Population - A Population is a collection of individuals people or items. Examples include manufactured items, plants, supermarkets, members of a club, etc. A population may be of finite size. For example, the number of of people within a certain area is finite as you are able to count how many people there are. On the other hand a population can also be considered to be of infinite size if its impossbile to know how many members there are within that population. For example, the number of grains of sand on a stretch of beach. It would be impossible to count them all.

Census - When information is obtained from all members of the population, the investigation would be known a census.

Sample - When you only use a certain number of members within the population, it is known as a sample.

Sample survey - An investigation using a sample is known as a sample survey.

Sampling frame - An ivestigation where each sampling unit is individually named or numbered, then the collective term for all the sampling units is sampling frame.

The concept of a statistic.

 

Anything that is measurable within a population can be called a population parameter. A parameter is a numerical property of a sample.

 

We find the population may be too large for us to calculate these parameters and so we take a random sample and estimate these parameters.

 

A statistic is a quantitiy calculated solely from the observations in a sample. It does not involve any unknown parameters.
